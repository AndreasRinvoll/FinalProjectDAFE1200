Our inspiration

The United Nations sustainability goal 3 has been our inspiration and focus for this project. It has a goal to “ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages” (4). In western countries like Norway, improved life-expectancy and general health in the populace has resulted in a growing percentage of seniors. This means that making society more accessible for this group is an important part of achieving this goal.

This project has also been inspired by our lectures this semester, which was focused on the importance of diversity in society. We believe that diversity means access to society for everyone, which these days also means being able to access and use modern technology. A group not being able to access such a big part of our lives hinders diversity (source elders and tech from innhold).

Why Kløpper

Loneliness among seniors in Norway

We wanted to make an app that was relevant and that solved a current problem. We wanted to make a difference, and therefore we focused on one of the groups that struggle the most with loneliness (1). In some studies, up to 40% of seniors reports feeling lonely (6).

The covid19-situation also made this a very relevant topic, as people are feeling more and more isolated these days due to increasing restrictions. Due to the pandemic, people have reduced their contact with friends, familiy and loved ones. This can put even more people at risk for experiencing loneliness.

Why accessability?


As people age, their vision, dexterity and memory grows less acute. This can make navigating websites and techical devices harder. While developers have gotten better over the last 11 years, they still have a way to go. Because websites are still substantially more difficult for older users to use (9).

Our Solution


Two of the solutions suggested by the WHO World Report on Ageing to tackle loneliness among older adults is to create opportunities for meaningful social roles and relationships, and to improve access to information and communications technologies (8). We have proposed a solution that is a mix of these two - to create a social platform for older adults, that is specifically designed to be user friendly for everyone, regardless of age.
