Sustainable Development Goal 3


To promote healthy lives and well-being for all



UN SDG 3 has a goal to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages (4). As the number of people aged 60+ is predicted to increase in the future, reaching this goal without addressing the specific needs of this group would be impossible (7). Among the issues relevant for this age group is loneliness. In some studies, up to 40% of older people report feeling lonely. (6) Loneliness has been shown to lead to overall worse health outcomes for the person affected - both physically and mentally (5).

Loneliness among seniors in Norway



According to Meld.St 19 (2014-2015), loneliness and lack of a support network among older adults also represents an important public health challenge in Norway (3). According to statistics from SSB, seniors are one of the groups experiencing the most loneliness, second only to young adults. Among those 67 years and older, nearly one in five of those asked had felt lonely at some point during the last two weeks (1). Among those 80+, the number of adults who report feeling lonely increases to three out of ten (2).

Our Solution



As people age, both their vision, dexterity and memory grows less acute (9). This can make navigating such things as low contrast websites, small fonts and elements, and confusing or unclear navigation systems difficult. While websites have gotten better over the last 11 years, they are still substantially more difficult for older users to use (9).

Two of the solutions suggested by the WHO World Report on Ageing to tackle loneliness among older adults is to create opportunities for meaningful social roles and relationships, and to improve access to information and communications technologies (8). We have proposed one solution that is a mix of these two - to create a social platform for older adults, that is specifically designed to be user friendly for everyone, regardless of age.
